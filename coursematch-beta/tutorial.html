Should I try to "game" the system? ‚ñº

No! The algorithm is strategy-proof. This means:

  • Ranking honestly is mathematically proven to be your best strategy
  • The algorithm was designed by Nobel laureate Alvin Roth specifically to prevent gaming
  • Trying to manipulate rankings will only hurt your chances
  • Students who rank strategically often end up in courses they don't want

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Gaming Mistakes to Avoid:

‚ùå Ranking an unpopular course as Favorite to "guarantee" a spot
‚ùå Not ranking competitive courses because you think you won't get them
‚ùå Ranking courses you don't want just because they're unpopular
‚ùå Trying to guess what others will rank

How do I diversify my course selection? ‚ñº

Create a balanced schedule by mixing different types of courses:

  • Favorite bucket: Your absolute must-have course
  • Great bucket: Courses you're genuinely excited about
  • Good bucket: Solid courses that fit your interests
  • Acceptable bucket: "Safety" options you'd be satisfied with

üìä Diversification Ideas:

Consider mixing:
‚Ä¢ Different subjects (Finance, Marketing, Strategy, Operations)
‚Ä¢ Different formats (Case-based, lectures, workshops)
‚Ä¢ Different workloads (Intensive vs. moderate)
‚Ä¢ Different meeting times (Spread throughout the week)

How do the ranking buckets work? ‚ñº

Course Match uses a bucket system with different priority weights. The official guide explains it like "floors of a building" for buckets and "steps on a staircase" for order within buckets:

  • ‚≠ê Favorite: Your #1 must-have course - only ONE allowed (highest utility)
  • ‚ú® Great: Courses you really want (significantly lower than Favorite)
  • üëç Good: Courses you'd be happy with (much lower than Great)
  • ‚úîÔ∏è Acceptable: Backup options you'd take if needed (lowest utility)

Example utility values: If courses are ranked one per bucket: 10,000 (Favorite) ‚Üí 1,000 (Great) ‚Üí 100 (Good) ‚Üí 10 (Acceptable). But if all in same bucket: 10,000 ‚Üí 9,500 ‚Üí 9,000 ‚Üí 8,500.

ü§î Understanding the System: Placing a course in "Great" means you'd prefer it over:

Just one course from "Good"
Several courses from "Good" combined
All courses in "Good"
What should go in my Favorite bucket? ‚ñº

Choose your Favorite carefully - you only get ONE:

  • Must be a course you absolutely need/want above all others
  • Could be a course that rarely appears or has limited seats
  • Could be a unique elective with a star professor
  • Should NOT be an "easy to get" course (waste of Favorite status)

üéØ Favorite Selection Checklist:

‚úì Is this course critical to your academic plan?
‚úì Would you be very disappointed without it?
‚úì Is it likely to be competitive?
If you answered "yes" to all three, it's a good Favorite choice!

How should I use the other buckets? ‚ñº

Strategic use of each bucket:

  • Great (3-6 courses): Courses you'd be genuinely excited about. Include both reaches and realistic options.
  • Good (4-8 courses): Solid courses that fit your schedule. Mix of different subjects/times.
  • Acceptable (3-6 courses): Safety net courses. Must be courses you'd actually attend!

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Warning

Never rank courses you wouldn't actually take! The algorithm might assign them to you.

How does the budget system work? ‚ñº

Each student has a budget to "purchase" courses based on clearing prices:

  • 1st year students: Have lower budgets
  • 2nd year students: Have higher budgets (includes leftover bid points from previous terms)
  • Tie-breakers: Small random variations added to break ties when students have identical preferences

‚ö†Ô∏è Key Point

You can't see your budget or clearing prices in advance. Just rank honestly - the algorithm handles the rest!

What are clearing prices? ‚ñº

Clearing prices are determined AFTER everyone submits rankings:

  • Based on supply (available seats) and demand (student preferences)
  • Popular courses have higher clearing prices
  • Less popular courses have lower clearing prices
  • The algorithm uses these prices to allocate courses fairly

üí° Strategy Tip

Ranking more courses gives you access to lower-priced options, helping you afford your top choices while filling your schedule.

What is "utility" in Course Match? ‚ñº

Utility represents your happiness or value from a schedule:

  • Course Match estimates utility from your rankings
  • Big utility jumps between buckets (Favorite ‚Üí Great ‚Üí Good)
  • Small utility changes within the same bucket
  • The algorithm tries to maximize your total utility within budget constraints

üìä Remember the Building Analogy:

‚Ä¢ Buckets = Different floors (big jumps)
‚Ä¢ Order within buckets = Steps on stairs (small changes)

How does the algorithm assign schedules? ‚ñº

The Course Match algorithm follows these steps:

  1. Creates all possible valid schedules for each student
  2. Orders them by utility (based on your rankings)
  3. Determines clearing prices based on overall demand
  4. Gives you the highest utility schedule you can afford
  5. Always tries to meet minimum credit requirements

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

If you use your Favorite bucket for a highly competitive course, the algorithm might spend most of your utility budget to get you that one course. This could mean you don't get a full schedule and will need to add courses during the add/drop period!

What are the biggest mistakes students make? ‚ñº

Avoid these common pitfalls:

  1. Not ranking enough courses - The #1 mistake!
  2. Waiting until the last minute - Submit 24 hours early to avoid system issues
  3. Wrong credit increments - Must use 1.5 credit increments (e.g., 12.0, 13.5, 15.0)
  4. Over-ranking courses - Don't rank 25+ courses if you only need 15 credits
  5. Leaving buckets empty - Use all buckets for flexibility
  6. Ranking courses you won't actually take - You might get stuck with them
  7. Ignoring time conflicts - The system doesn't check your calendar
  8. Forgetting to submit - Changes save, but you must click submit!
  9. Not checking prerequisites - Make sure you're eligible

How can I estimate course popularity? ‚ñº

While exact demand is hidden, you can make educated guesses:

  • High demand indicators:
    • Star professors (check evaluations in course pages)
    • Prime time slots (Tuesday/Thursday afternoons)
    • New or trending topics
    • Small class size limits
  • Lower demand indicators:
    • 9:00 AM or 6:00 PM course times
    • Highly specialized topics
    • Many prerequisites
    • Large lecture halls
Can I coordinate with friends? ‚ñº

Course Match doesn't have a way to directly link registrations with friends:

  • You can't "link" registrations or guarantee being together
  • Each student's preferences and utility budget are different
  • Course allocation depends on demand, capacity, and individual budgets
  • Even if you rank the same courses, outcomes may differ
  • Popular courses may only have room for some of your group

üë• Reality Check:

‚Ä¢ Focus on courses YOU want to take
‚Ä¢ Each person should rank based on their own preferences
‚Ä¢ Compare schedules after results are released
‚Ä¢ Consider coordinating during add/drop period
‚Ä¢ Remember: Different preferences = different results

What happens after ranking closes? ‚ñº

The post-ranking timeline at Columbia Business School:

  • Processing: Takes 1-3 weeks (varies by term)
  • Results released: Check your assigned schedule via email notification
  • Add/Drop period: Check the official calendar for dates
  • Waitlists: Join strategically (up to 27 credits enrolled + waitlisted combined)
  • Shopping period: Attend first classes even if waitlisted

üìÖ Post-Results Action Plan:

‚úì Screenshot your assigned schedule immediately
‚úì Check for any time conflicts or issues
‚úì Join waitlists for desired courses
‚úì Remember: 27 credit limit for enrolled + waitlisted
‚úì Attend ALL first classes (assigned + waitlisted)
‚úì Have Add/Drop backup plans ready

What are "Rules" and how do I use them? ‚ñº

Rules tell Course Match which courses you consider substitutes. Use them when you only want one course from a group, even if ranked highly.

  • Example: Multiple sections of the same course
  • Example: Similar electives where you only need one
  • You can set to take as few as 1 or as many as 3 from a group

üìù How to Create Rules:

1. Rank your courses normally first
2. Click "Add Rule" button at bottom of ranking page
3. Select which courses are substitutes
4. Choose how many you want (1-3)

Why didn't I get my Favorite but my friend who ranked it lower did? ‚ñº

Most likely your friend could afford it but you couldn't:

  • Your friend may have had a larger budget or tie-breaker
  • Their Favorite course may have had lower demand (cheaper)
  • They may have ranked more courses, giving more flexibility

üîç Another Possibility:

If you didn't rank enough courses, Course Match couldn't create a complete schedule with that course within your budget. Always rank 2-4x what you need!

Any final pro tips? ‚ñº

Advanced strategies from successful students:

  • Time diversity: Rank courses at different times to avoid conflicts
  • Subject diversity: Mix quantitative and qualitative courses
  • Professor research: Check teaching ratings and styles
  • Workload balance: Mix intensive and lighter courses
  • Network effect: Consider which courses alumni recommend

üí´ Remember: Be Fair to Yourself

Course Match creates fair schedules, but you need to be realistic too. You won't get all the popular courses - everyone wants them! Create a balanced mix of highly competitive courses and solid alternatives. The algorithm works best when you give it good options to work with.

What is Course Match? ‚ñº

Course Match is Columbia Business School's fair course allocation system based on Nobel Prize-winning economic theory. Unlike "first-come, first-served" registration:

  • Everyone submits preferences during the same window
  • An algorithm assigns courses to maximize overall student satisfaction
  • Your chances depend on your preferences and course popularity, not registration speed
  • The system is specifically designed to reward honest ranking

When can I submit my rankings? ‚ñº

The ranking window for Columbia Business School typically:

  • Opens several days before the term begins
  • Remains open for 5-7 days
  • Closes at 4:00 PM EST on the deadline
  • Allows unlimited changes until the deadline
  • Only counts your final submission

Do NOT wait until deadline day! Last-minute submissions risk:

  • System overload or crashes
  • No time to fix technical issues
  • No support available if problems occur
  • Missing the 4:00 PM deadline entirely

2. Two days before deadline: Review and adjust rankings

How should I select my target credits? ‚ñº

IMPORTANT: Select your target credits in 1.5 credit increments only!

Valid options: 1.5, 3.0, 4.5, 6.0, 7.5, 9.0, 10.5, 12.0, 13.5, 15.0, 16.5, 18.0

üìê Why 1.5 increments?

Most CBS courses are either 1.5 or 3.0 credits. The system requires these increments to ensure valid schedule combinations.

  • Full-time students: Typically target 12-15 credits
How many courses should I rank? ‚ñº

This is the #1 most important rule: Rank 2x the number of credits you need!

  • If you need 15 credits ‚Üí Rank about 30 credits worth of courses
  • If you need 12 credits ‚Üí Rank about 24 credits worth
  • If you need 6 credits ‚Üí Rank about 12 credits worth

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid Over-Ranking!

Don't rank more than 25 courses. If you're taking 15 credits, ranking 30 credits worth makes sense. But ranking 97 choices (120+ credits) creates unnecessary overlap and confusion.

Be strategic: Focus on courses you genuinely want to take. Quality over quantity!

‚úÖ Smart Approach

  • Rank courses you're genuinely interested in
  • Include some "safety" options you'd be happy with
  • Don't just add courses to hit a number
  • Remember: Ranking more courses does NOT hurt your chances for top picks

üìå Important Note on Favorite Bucket

  • If you use your Favorite bucket, make sure it's a course you REALLY want
  • The system may use most of your utility budget to get you that one course
  • This could mean not getting a full schedule if your Favorite is highly competitive
  • Consider: Is this course worth potentially sacrificing other courses?

What if I have registration holds? ‚ñº

Registration holds prevent you from accessing Course Match. Common holds include:

  • Immunization holds: Submit health forms to Student Health
  • Financial holds: Contact Student Financial Services
  • Academic holds: Meet with your academic advisor
  • International student holds: Check with ISSO for visa compliance

Important: Resolve holds BEFORE the ranking window opens, as they can take 24-48 hours to clear in the system.

Why can't I see a certain course in Course Match? ‚ñº

There are several reasons why a course might not appear in your Course Match options:

  • Time conflict with core courses: Check the course pages for schedule details - courses that conflict with your pre-enrolled cores won't appear
  • Application-only course: Some courses require direct application to the Center/Program before Course Match
  • Not open to your program: The course may be restricted to specific programs (Full-time, EMBA, etc.)
  • Prerequisites not met: You may need to complete certain courses first

Good news: Even if not available in Course Match, the course might open during Add/Drop pending space availability!

üìä Understanding Course Demand Ratios

Before viewing the course data, it's important to understand what these numbers mean:

Example: Applied Value Investing
121 students wanted this course
Only 10 seats were available
Demand Ratio = 12.1:1

What does 12.1:1 mean?

For every 1 seat available, 12.1 students wanted it. In other words, roughly 12 students competed for each seat.

How to interpret demand ratios:

  • 10:1 or higher = Extremely competitive (only ~10% get in)
  • 5:1 to 10:1 = Very competitive (10-20% get in)
  • 2:1 to 5:1 = Moderately competitive (20-50% get in)
  • Under 2:1 = Good odds (50%+ get in)

Why this matters:

When you see a course with a 12:1 ratio marked as "Favorite" by 50% of students, you understand that:

  • It's extremely competitive
  • Most students who rank it won't get in
  • Using your Favorite bucket here is a big gamble
  • You need strong alternatives in other buckets

Use this data to make informed, strategic ranking decisions!
